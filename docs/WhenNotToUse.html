When Not To Use RCFD‑PCG

RCFD‑PCG targets tall‑skinny least‑squares where a ridge‑aware sketch yields fewer CG iterations and stable wall‑clock wins. It’s not the best tool everywhere.

Use a baseline (LSQR/LSMR/QR) when: - Small dense problems (low d): direct QR/Cholesky is usually fastest. - Well‑conditioned systems with modest d: RCFD cuts iterations but sketch overhead may dominate. - Non tall‑skinny or poorly formed inputs: prefer methods tailored to the regime.

What the tool does for you: - Auto bail‑out: if heuristics detect a well‑conditioned case, the CLI/API can skip preconditioning and return the baseline result. - Honest reports: JSON/HTML report includes whether bail‑out occurred and the baseline timing.

Tips: - For repeated solves (multiple b or runs), RCFD’s preconditioner reuse typically flips wall‑clock in your favor. - For very wide d, consider --precond-type blockdiag --num-col-blocks K to keep factorization fast.
